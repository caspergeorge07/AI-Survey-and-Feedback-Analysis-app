**Version:** 0.2  
**Status:** Draft  
**Owner:** Brand and Product Marketing

## Purpose

This document defines SurveyIQ's brand guidelines for product, website, reports, sales, support, documentation, and AI-generated messaging. It ensures SurveyIQ presents a consistent, professional, trustworthy identity as an AI-powered survey and qualitative feedback intelligence platform.

SurveyIQ's brand should communicate that the product turns free-text survey responses into executive-ready business insights with clarity, structure, and confidence.

## 1. Mission

SurveyIQ exists to help organizations understand what people are saying, why it matters, and what leaders should do next.

The mission is to make qualitative feedback analysis:

- Faster for analysts.
- Clearer for managers.
- More actionable for executives.
- Easier to communicate through professional reports.

## 2. Vision

SurveyIQ is an AI-powered survey and qualitative feedback intelligence platform that transforms free-text survey responses into executive-ready business insights.

The long-term vision is to become the trusted intelligence layer for customer, employee, product, and market feedback.

## 3. Brand Positioning

SurveyIQ sits between survey tools, spreadsheets, research operations, and executive reporting.

Positioning statement:

**SurveyIQ turns raw survey comments into clear themes, sentiment, risks, recommended actions, and management-ready reports.**

SurveyIQ is not:

- A generic AI chat product.
- A survey collection tool.
- A spreadsheet replacement for every workflow.
- A black-box research automation tool.

SurveyIQ is:

- A qualitative feedback intelligence platform.
- A structured AI analysis workflow.
- A reporting system for decision-makers.
- A bridge between raw feedback and business action.

## 4. Brand Personality

SurveyIQ should feel:

- **Intelligent:** understands messy qualitative feedback.
- **Professional:** suitable for executive and enterprise environments.
- **Clear:** reduces ambiguity rather than adding complexity.
- **Trustworthy:** transparent about AI outputs and limitations.
- **Practical:** focused on decisions, reports, and action.
- **Calm:** avoids hype-heavy or overly casual messaging.

## 5. Tone of Voice

SurveyIQ's voice is confident, concise, and evidence-led.

Tone by context:

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Product UI | Clear, direct, calm |
| Analysis results | Analytical, balanced, evidence-led |
| Executive reports | Polished, concise, management-ready |
| Error states | Helpful, specific, reassuring |
| Marketing | Confident, outcome-focused |
| Security | Precise, conservative, trustworthy |
| Documentation | Practical, structured, complete |

## 6. Writing Style

Writing rules:

- Use plain English.
- Prefer active voice.
- Keep sentences concise.
- Use business language without jargon overload.
- Be specific about outputs: themes, sentiment, risks, actions, reports.
- Avoid unsupported claims.
- Avoid vague AI hype.
- Use "AI-powered" where relevant, but do not make every sentence about AI.

Preferred:

- "Upload a CSV or Excel file to identify themes, sentiment, and recommended actions."
- "Analysis failed because the selected column does not contain enough text responses."
- "Negative sentiment is concentrated around onboarding and support response times."

Avoid:

- "Unlock revolutionary insights with next-generation AI."
- "The AI knows exactly what your customers want."
- "Something broke."

## 7. Logo Usage

Formal logo assets are not yet defined. Until the logo system is finalized:

- Use the SurveyIQ wordmark consistently.
- Do not stretch, distort, recolor, or add effects to the wordmark.
- Use sufficient contrast against backgrounds.
- Keep clear space around the wordmark.
- Use "SurveyIQ" with capital S, I, and Q.

Future logo guidance should define:

- Primary logo.
- Reversed logo.
- Icon mark.
- Minimum size.
- Clear space.
- Incorrect usage.

## 8. Colour Usage

SurveyIQ uses a professional blue, white, and neutral grey palette with restrained semantic accents.

Rules:

- Use blue for primary actions and navigation selection.
- Use neutral backgrounds for dashboards and app shell.
- Use green for positive sentiment and successful completion.
- Use red for negative sentiment, risk, or destructive actions.
- Use amber for warnings and partial completion.
- Use purple only as a restrained AI feature accent.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.

Refer to [05_Design_System.md](05_Design_System.md) for token-level colour guidance.

## 9. Typography

Typography should feel modern, readable, and businesslike.

Rules:

- Use system fonts or an approved enterprise SaaS font family.
- Use clear hierarchy for page titles, section headings, cards, body text, labels, and metadata.
- Keep report typography polished but not decorative.
- Avoid overly playful display fonts.
- Use sentence case for most UI labels and headings.

## 10. Iconography

Iconography should be:

- Simple.
- Line-based or similarly restrained.
- Consistent in stroke and size.
- Paired with labels for unfamiliar actions.

Preferred icon concepts:

- Upload.
- Download.
- Report.
- Chart.
- Search.
- Filter.
- Settings.
- Warning.
- Success.
- AI Assistant.

Do not use icons as the only indicator of sentiment or risk.

## 11. Illustration Style

Illustrations should support comprehension, not decoration.

Use illustration for:

- Public-site workflow explanations.
- Empty dashboard first actions.
- Sample report previews.
- Light onboarding guidance.

Avoid:

- Cartoon-like mascots.
- Decorative background blobs.
- Busy abstract AI imagery.
- Illustrations in dense analytics views where charts or data are more useful.

## 12. Photography Style

Photography is optional and should be used carefully.

If photography is used:

- Choose professional workplace or research contexts.
- Avoid generic stock-photo stereotypes.
- Avoid dark, blurred, or overly atmospheric images.
- Use images that support real product value or customer context.

The product itself should usually be shown through screenshots, report previews, and data visualizations rather than lifestyle photography.

## 13. UI Writing

UI copy should help users complete tasks without overexplaining.

Rules:

- Button labels should be action-oriented: "Upload file", "Run analysis", "Generate report".
- Form labels should be explicit: "Feedback column", "Project name".
- Helper text should clarify risk or decision points.
- Avoid showing implementation details unless needed for troubleshooting.
- Keep AI explanations concise and focused on what the user can do next.

## 14. Empty State Copy

Empty states should be action-first and reassuring.

Pattern:

1. Clear title.
2. One sentence explaining the state.
3. Primary action.
4. Optional secondary action.

Examples:

- **No analyses yet**  
  Upload your first survey export to generate themes, sentiment, and an executive report.

- **No reports yet**  
  Generate a report from a completed analysis to create a management-ready PDF.

- **No projects yet**  
  Create a project to organize analyses, uploaded files, and reports.

## 15. Error Copy

Error copy should explain what happened and how to recover.

Rules:

- Be specific.
- Avoid blame.
- Avoid raw stack traces.
- Avoid exposing secrets or internal paths.
- Say whether retry is appropriate.
- Preserve trust by explaining what was saved where possible.

Examples:

- "We could not read this file. Upload a CSV or Excel file with visible column headers."
- "Select a feedback column before starting analysis."
- "Analysis stopped because one batch could not be completed. Retry the analysis or reduce the file size."
- "The OpenAI API key was not detected. Add `OPENAI_API_KEY` to the backend environment and restart the backend."

## 16. Success Messages

Success messages should confirm completion and guide the next action.

Examples:

- "File uploaded. Review the preview and select the feedback column."
- "Analysis complete. Review themes, sentiment, risks, and recommended actions."
- "Report generated. Preview the PDF or download it now."
- "CSV download is ready."

Keep success messages brief. Avoid exaggerated celebration.

## 17. AI Messaging Principles

SurveyIQ should describe AI as a powerful assistant to analysis, not as an unquestionable authority.

Rules:

- Be transparent when content is AI-generated.
- Use confidence scores to indicate uncertainty.
- Encourage review of low-confidence outputs.
- Avoid claiming the AI "knows" intent.
- Avoid unsupported predictions.
- Use "suggests", "indicates", or "is associated with" when evidence is inferential.
- Tie insights back to themes, counts, sentiment, or example responses.

Preferred:

- "The analysis suggests onboarding is a recurring source of frustration."
- "Low-confidence responses may need manual review."

Avoid:

- "The AI has discovered the exact cause."
- "Customers will churn unless this is fixed."

## 18. Marketing Voice

Marketing copy should be outcome-led and credible.

Messaging priorities:

- Move from raw comments to executive-ready insight.
- Save analyst time.
- Improve consistency of qualitative analysis.
- Make reports easier to share with leadership.
- Highlight themes, sentiment, risks, recommendations, CSV, and PDF outputs.

Avoid overclaiming automation. SurveyIQ assists business insight work; it should not imply humans are unnecessary.

## 19. Website Voice

Website copy should help prospects understand quickly:

- What SurveyIQ does.
- Who it is for.
- What the workflow looks like.
- What outputs they receive.
- Why they can trust it.

Preferred structure:

- Clear headline.
- Practical supporting copy.
- Product/report preview.
- Use cases.
- CTA to start or book demo.

## 20. Sales Voice

Sales messaging should be consultative and business-focused.

Emphasize:

- Current analysis pain.
- Time saved.
- Executive-ready reporting.
- Repeatable workflow.
- Trust, security, and future governance.

Avoid:

- Technical over-detail before buyer need is clear.
- Claims about compliance certifications not yet achieved.
- Enterprise promises not yet in the roadmap.

## 21. Documentation Voice

Documentation should be:

- Direct.
- Complete.
- Structured.
- Actionable.
- Accurate to the current product stage.

Documentation should clearly distinguish:

- Current MVP.
- Near-term roadmap.
- Future enterprise capabilities.

## 22. Accessibility Writing

Accessible writing rules:

- Use clear labels for buttons, links, and fields.
- Avoid "click here"; describe the destination or action.
- Use headings in logical order.
- Write alt text that explains function or meaning.
- Do not rely on visual location only, such as "use the button on the right".
- Explain errors in text, not color alone.

## 23. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Do write with clarity and confidence.
- Do connect insights to evidence.
- Do use management-ready language in reports.
- Do explain AI limitations when relevant.
- Do keep user actions obvious.
- Do keep the product calm and professional.

### Don't

- Don't use hype-heavy AI claims.
- Don't imply perfect accuracy.
- Don't bury the primary action.
- Don't expose secrets, file paths, or raw technical errors.
- Don't use color alone for sentiment or risk.
- Don't introduce product promises outside the approved roadmap.

## 24. Future Brand Evolution

Future brand work should define:

- Logo and icon system.
- Formal typography selection.
- Approved screenshot style.
- Report cover and PDF visual system.
- Public-site imagery direction.
- Customer story format.
- Trust center messaging.
- Enterprise procurement language.

Brand evolution should preserve the core SurveyIQ identity: professional, analytical, trustworthy, and action-oriented.
