## 1. Product Vision

SurveyIQ is an AI-powered survey and qualitative feedback intelligence platform that transforms free-text survey responses into executive-ready business insights.

The platform helps teams move from raw qualitative feedback to clear themes, sentiment, risks, recommended actions, and professional management reports without requiring manual coding, spreadsheet-heavy analysis, or specialist research operations.

## 2. Product Mission

SurveyIQ exists to help organizations understand what people are saying, why it matters, and what leaders should do next.

The mission is to make qualitative feedback analysis:

- Faster for analysts.
- Clearer for managers.
- More actionable for executives.
- Easier to communicate through polished reports.

## 3. Target Users

### Primary Users

- Customer experience teams.
- Product managers.
- Research and insights teams.
- Employee experience and HR teams.
- Operations leaders.

### Secondary Users

- Executives consuming reports.
- Team managers reviewing trends.
- Administrators managing workspaces and access.

## 4. Core Problem

Organizations collect large amounts of free-text feedback, but the analysis process is often slow, inconsistent, and difficult to convert into management-ready outputs.

Common pain points:

- Manual theme coding takes too long.
- Sentiment interpretation is inconsistent.
- Spreadsheets do not communicate insights well.
- Executives need concise conclusions, not raw data.
- Teams struggle to turn feedback into recommended actions.

## 5. Product Promise

SurveyIQ turns survey comments and qualitative responses into:

- Clear themes.
- Sentiment summaries.
- Confidence-rated response analysis.
- Executive summaries.
- Key risks.
- Recommended actions.
- Downloadable CSV outputs.
- Professional PDF management reports.

## 6. Differentiation

SurveyIQ is focused on executive-ready qualitative intelligence rather than generic text processing.

Differentiators:

- Multi-stage AI analysis workflow.
- Canonical theme consolidation.
- Management report generation.
- CSV and PDF export.
- Local MVP path that can evolve into a SaaS platform.
- Clear separation between raw analysis, insight generation, and reporting.

## 7. Guiding Principles

- Preserve trust: never expose secrets or raw data unnecessarily.
- Preserve compatibility: avoid breaking existing workflows and exports.
- Make insights actionable: every analysis should help a user decide what to do next.
- Design for clarity: executives should understand the result without reading every response.
- Keep the MVP focused: avoid authentication, billing, teams, and enterprise complexity until the core workflow is strong.
- Build toward scale: structure analysis and reporting so larger files can be handled safely.

## 8. Success Measures

Initial success indicators:

- Users can upload CSV or Excel files successfully.
- Users can select a free-text feedback column.
- Analysis completes reliably for small and larger survey files.
- Theme and sentiment counts are clear.
- CSV exports remain available.
- PDF reports are suitable for management review.
- Users can understand key risks and recommended actions without manual rework.

Future SaaS success indicators:

- Time saved compared with manual analysis.
- Report download rate.
- Repeat project usage.
- Team adoption across departments.
- Reduction in manual theme editing.
- Customer willingness to use reports in leadership meetings.

## 9. Scope Boundaries

Current MVP scope:

- File upload.
- Data preview.
- Feedback column selection.
- AI analysis.
- Theme and sentiment counts.
- CSV export.
- PDF management report export.
- Local file handling.

Explicitly out of current scope:

- Authentication.
- Payments.
- Subscriptions.
- Databases.
- Team collaboration.
- Enterprise governance.
